# Collatz as Native Language

Version: v3.1

This document defines Collatz as a native dynamical language.

## Claim

Collatz is not first an algorithm to solve.

Collatz is first a language generated by a transformation rule.

The language is not spoken in human concepts such as solution, proof, or termination.

Those are later translations.

The native language appears through generated structure.

## Basic grammar

The current native grammar contains:

expansion
discharge
debt
shadow
regeneration
compensation
closure
obstruction

## Grammar meaning

Expansion is upward pressure.

Discharge is binary removal.

Debt is insufficient discharge after expansion.

Shadow is persistence of prior debt influence.

Regeneration is renewed debt after apparent compensation.

Compensation is later discharge repairing prior debt.

Closure is erasure of obstruction potential.

Obstruction is self-preserving unclosed debt.

## Collatz speaking

Collatz speaks through the repeated production, conservation, loss, and transformation of these objects.

The task is not to force Collatz to answer:

Do you reach 1?

The task is first to ask:

What are you producing?

What are you preserving?

What are you unable to preserve?

## Native object

A Collatz trajectory is not merely a path to 1.

A Collatz trajectory is a generated structural sentence in the language of expansion and discharge.

## Translation boundary

Classical notation may describe this language only after the native structure is visible.

The translation must not erase the native meaning.
