# Dangerous Instability

Version: v3.7

Dangerous instability means dangerous grammar appears but fails to preserve itself.

It mutates, releases, or transitions away.

## Meaning

This is important because it may show that Collatz can speak dangerous grammar without conserving obstruction.

## Not a proof

Dangerous instability in a bounded atlas does not prove obstruction impossible.

It only says that in the observed bounded grammar, dangerous forms did not remain obstruction-stable.

## Native reading

Dangerous instability means:

Collatz can generate danger without keeping danger as a stable self-preserving form.
