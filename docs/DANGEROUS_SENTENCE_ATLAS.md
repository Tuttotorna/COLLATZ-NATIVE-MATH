# Dangerous Sentence Atlas

Version: v3.4

Dangerous sentences are native sentences where stress appears close to the obstruction boundary.

They may include:

- dangerous regeneration
- tight positive surplus
- high recovery stress
- many debt windows
- persistent shadow signals
- near-obstruction grammar profiles

## Dangerous is not obstruction

Dangerous regeneration is not obstruction.

A tight surplus is not obstruction.

High recovery stress is not obstruction.

These are stress forms.

## Native role

Dangerous sentences are important because they tell us where Collatz speaks closest to the edge of non-closure.

The goal is not to prove from them.

The goal is to hear their structure clearly.
