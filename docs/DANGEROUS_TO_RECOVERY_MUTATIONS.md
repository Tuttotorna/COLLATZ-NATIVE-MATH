# Dangerous-to-Recovery Mutations

Version: v3.6

A dangerous-to-recovery mutation occurs when a dangerous native sentence changes into a non-obstruction sentence.

## Meaning

This is not proof.

But it is structurally important.

It says that dangerous grammar can release into non-obstruction grammar.

## What this may suggest

It may suggest that some dangerous forms are unstable as obstruction carriers.

But v3.6 does not claim that all dangerous forms must release.

## Forbidden interpretation

Do not say:

Dangerous forms always recover.

Do not say:

Obstruction is impossible.

Do not say:

Collatz is solved.

## Correct interpretation

The bounded mutation atlas observed dangerous-release behavior in the selected sentence set.
