# Danger-Release Invariant Candidates

Version: v3.9

Danger-release invariant candidates are conserved cases where dangerous grammar releases instead of preserving obstruction.

## Meaning

This is important.

It says that release behavior may be a recurring native form.

## Boundary

Danger-release invariant candidates do not prove global closure.

They do not prove obstruction impossible.

They do not solve Collatz.

They only mark behavior that deserves stronger native testing.
