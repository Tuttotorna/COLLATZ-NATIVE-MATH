# Hardness and Obstruction Lemma Candidates

Version: v2.2

This document introduces lemma candidates separating hardness from obstruction.

## Lemma candidate 1: hardness measures stress

Native object:

hardness

Candidate statement:

A hardness metric identifies stress inside a trajectory structure.

Native meaning retained:

hardness is structural pressure, not failure.

Status:

stable boundary lemma candidate.

## Lemma candidate 2: hardness is not obstruction

Native object:

obstruction

Candidate statement:

High hardness is not sufficient to identify obstruction.

Native meaning retained:

obstruction requires self-preserving unclosed debt.

Status:

stable boundary lemma candidate.

## Lemma candidate 3: long trajectory is not obstruction

Native object:

long trajectory

Candidate statement:

A long trajectory is not sufficient to identify native obstruction.

Native meaning retained:

duration is not persistence of unclosed debt.

Status:

stable boundary lemma candidate.

## Lemma candidate 4: tight surplus is not obstruction

Native object:

tight surplus

Candidate statement:

A tight positive surplus identifies near-obstruction stress, but does not imply obstruction if compensation remains positive and no self-preserving unclosed debt is detected.

Native meaning retained:

near failure is not failure.

Status:

stable boundary lemma candidate.

## Lemma candidate 5: obstruction requires persistence

Native object:

obstruction candidate

Candidate statement:

A native obstruction candidate requires persistent debt, non-erased shadow, dangerous regeneration, insufficient compensation, and no closure event.

Native meaning retained:

obstruction is persistent unclosed debt.

Status:

open lemma candidate.

## Current status

The hardness-obstruction separation is stable as a boundary.

The full obstruction lemma remains open until shadow and regeneration are made stronger.

Hardness measures stress.
