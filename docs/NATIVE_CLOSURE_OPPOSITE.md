# Native Closure Opposite

Version: v1.7

A closure condition is meaningful only if its opposite is explicit.

This document defines the opposite of native closure.

## Native closure

Native closure would mean:

    Every admissible debt structure eventually forces compensating discharge sufficient to erase its obstruction potential.

## Opposite of native closure

The opposite is:

    There exists an admissible debt structure that persists, regenerates, and avoids compensating discharge indefinitely.

This is the native obstruction.

## Closure is not reaching 1

Reaching 1 is the classical terminal framing.

Native closure is earlier and structural.

A trajectory may show closure behavior before the final terminal state.

The native method studies whether the mechanism of closure is forced, not merely whether a computation reaches 1.

## Closure failure

Closure failure requires more than delayed recovery.

Closure failure requires:

- persistent debt
- non-erased shadow
- regeneration
- no sufficient discharge
- no forced surplus
- no structural collapse

## Why this matters

Without defining the opposite of closure, the native method cannot become rigorous.

It would only describe observed behavior.

With the opposite defined, the research can ask:

    Can the opposite of closure exist?

That is the correct native path.
