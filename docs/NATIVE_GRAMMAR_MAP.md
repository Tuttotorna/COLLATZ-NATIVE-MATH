# Native Grammar Map

Version: v3.2

This document defines the native grammar map of Collatz.

The purpose is not to solve Collatz.

The purpose is not to prove termination.

The purpose is to describe what Collatz generates when it is not forced immediately into the human terminal question.

The central question is:

What is Collatz before it becomes a conjecture?

## Core position

Collatz is treated here as a native dynamical language.

A trajectory is not first read as a path toward 1.

A trajectory is first read as a generated sentence made from structural objects:

- expansion
- discharge
- debt
- shadow
- regeneration
- compensation
- closure
- obstruction

## Grammar before proof

The native grammar comes before proof.

The grammar asks:

- what Collatz produces
- what Collatz preserves
- what Collatz regenerates
- what Collatz discharges
- what Collatz erases
- what Collatz fails to preserve
- what Collatz permits as obstruction

Proof is a later translation layer.

## Native grammar map

The current native grammar map is:

expansion creates pressure

discharge responds to pressure

weak discharge generates debt

debt may leave shadow

shadow may carry unresolved danger

compensation repairs debt

regeneration renews debt after apparent repair

benign regeneration is renewed debt that is compensated

dangerous regeneration is renewed debt with persistent shadow or weakening compensation

closure erases obstruction potential

obstruction is self-preserving unclosed debt

## Correct posture

Do not solve first.

Describe first.

Do not force Collatz to justify itself as a conjecture before understanding it as a native dynamical object.

## Status

This is not a proof of the Collatz conjecture.

This is a native grammar layer.
