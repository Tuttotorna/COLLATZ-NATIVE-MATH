# Native Grammar Objects

Version: v3.2

This document defines the grammar objects used in the native reading of Collatz.

These are not theorem objects yet.

They are grammar objects.

## expansion

Expansion is the upward pressure produced by the odd transformation.

Native role:

expansion introduces structural pressure.

## discharge

Discharge is the binary removal after expansion.

Native role:

discharge releases pressure.

## debt

Debt is generated when discharge is insufficient relative to expansion pressure.

Native role:

debt records unresolved pressure.

## shadow

Shadow is the persistence of prior debt after apparent local recovery.

Native role:

shadow carries unresolved danger.

## regeneration

Regeneration is the reappearance of debt after compensation.

Native role:

regeneration tests whether debt can renew itself.

## compensation

Compensation is later discharge that repairs prior debt.

Native role:

compensation reduces or erases debt pressure.

## closure

Closure is the erasure of obstruction potential.

Native role:

closure means debt cannot continue as self-preserving obstruction.

## obstruction

Obstruction is persistent unclosed debt.

Native role:

obstruction is debt that survives discharge, shadow, regeneration, and compensation without closure.

## Important distinction

These grammar objects are not introduced to prove Collatz.

They are introduced to let Collatz describe its own generated structure.
