# Native Sentence Atlas

Version: v3.4

The Native Sentence Atlas maps the sentence forms currently extracted from Collatz-native observations.

The purpose is not to solve Collatz.

The purpose is to listen to what Collatz produces as a native dynamical language.

## Core rule

No solution before native language.

## What the atlas asks

The atlas asks:

What sentence forms does Collatz produce before it is translated into a terminal conjecture?

This is different from asking whether every trajectory reaches 1.

The atlas does not begin from termination.

It begins from generated native sentences.

## What counts as a native sentence

A native sentence is a trajectory-level utterance made from native grammar objects:

- expansion
- discharge
- debt
- shadow
- regeneration
- compensation
- closure
- obstruction

A trajectory is treated as an utterance of this grammar.

## What the atlas records

The atlas records:

- sentence classes
- sentence families
- native word counts
- dangerous sentence counts
- obstruction sentence counts
- strongest recovery-stress sentences
- tightest surplus sentences
- dominant grammar profiles
- rare grammar profiles
- missing sentence classes

## Boundary

The atlas is not a proof layer.

No obstruction sentence detected in the atlas is not proof that obstruction sentences cannot exist.

The atlas is a language map.
