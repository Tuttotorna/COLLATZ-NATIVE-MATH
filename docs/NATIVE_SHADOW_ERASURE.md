# Native Shadow Erasure

Version: v1.8

This document defines shadow erasure.

Shadow is the persistence of prior debt after apparent compensation.

## Shadow

A debt shadow exists when a previous debt structure still influences later trajectory behavior.

The shadow is not the debt itself.
It is the remaining structural trace of the debt.

## Why shadow matters

A bad window can be compensated locally while still leaving shadow.

If shadow remains capable of generating new obstruction potential, closure has not yet been established.

## Shadow erasure

Shadow erasure occurs when the prior debt no longer acts as a carrier for future obstruction.

A shadow is erased when:

1. the original bad window is compensated;
2. later structure does not preserve the debt pattern;
3. regeneration, if present, is compensated;
4. no self-preserving obstruction line remains.

## Shadow persistence

Shadow persists when:

- dangerous structure reappears;
- regeneration becomes harder to compensate;
- surplus margins repeatedly shrink;
- the same debt pattern continues through later blocks;
- compensation never destroys the obstruction potential.

## Important distinction

Shadow erasure is not identical to reaching 1.

A trajectory may show shadow erasure before the terminal endpoint.

The native method studies closure before terminality.
