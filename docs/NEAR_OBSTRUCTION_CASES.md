# Near Obstruction Cases

Version: v2.7

This document defines near-obstruction cases.

A near-obstruction case is not an obstruction.

A near-obstruction case is a stress case that approaches the obstruction signature but remains compensated or undecided within the bounded protocol.

## Near-obstruction indicators

Indicators include:

- very high recovery stress;
- very tight positive surplus;
- high debt-window count;
- high regeneration count;
- high dangerous-regeneration count;
- long post-recovery distance.

## Boundary

Near obstruction does not mean obstruction.

Near failure does not mean failure.

Tight compensation does not mean compensation failure.

## Purpose

Near-obstruction cases are useful because they show where the native method should focus next.

They should guide expanded search and shadow persistence instrumentation.
