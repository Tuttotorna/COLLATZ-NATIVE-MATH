# Rare Native Sentences

Version: v3.4

Rare native sentences are sentence profiles that appear only a small number of times in the observed atlas.

They matter because they may reveal boundary behavior.

## Rare does not mean proof

A rare sentence is not proof.

A rare sentence is not obstruction.

A rare sentence is a signal that Collatz produced a less common grammatical form.

## What rare sentences can show

Rare sentences can show:

- unusual recovery pressure
- unusual surplus tightness
- unusual regeneration shape
- unusual debt concentration
- possible boundary forms
- missing or unstable grammar transitions

## Correct role

Rare sentences should guide the next grammar map.

They should not be forced into theorem language.

## Native question

The question is not:

Does this rare case prove something?

The question is:

What kind of sentence did Collatz just speak?
