# Recurrence as Native Behavior

Version: v3.5

Recurrence is treated as native behavior.

It is not treated as theorem evidence.

## Why recurrence matters

A language is not understood only by listing words.

It is understood by seeing how words return, combine, and mutate.

The same applies here.

The v3.4 atlas lists sentence forms.

v3.5 studies how those sentence forms recur.

## Native recurrence questions

The recurrence layer asks:

- What follows a dangerous sentence?
- Does a recovery sentence stay recovery-like nearby?
- Do rare sentences remain rare?
- Do dangerous sentences cluster?
- Does obstruction-like grammar recur?
- Does stress become stable or dissolve?

## Forbidden translation

Do not translate recurrence into proof.

Do not translate repeated recovery into global closure.

Do not translate absence of obstruction recurrence into impossibility.

## Correct reading

Recurrence tells us how Collatz speaks across nearby sentence forms.
