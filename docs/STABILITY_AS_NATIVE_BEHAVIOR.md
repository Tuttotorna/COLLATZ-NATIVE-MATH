# Stability as Native Behavior

Version: v3.7

Stability is not proof.

Stability is native behavior.

A grammar becomes legible when we can tell which forms persist and which forms mutate.

## Three layers

Recurrence means return.

Mutation means transformation.

Stability means resistance to transformation.

## Native question

The question is:

What does Collatz keep?

Not:

How do we force Collatz to end?

## Why this matters

If dangerous forms are unstable, then danger may be part of Collatz grammar without being obstruction.

If dangerous forms are stable, then the grammar may contain deeper obstruction-like structures.

v3.7 maps this distinction.
