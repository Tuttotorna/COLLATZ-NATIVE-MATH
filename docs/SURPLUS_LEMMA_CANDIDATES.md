# Surplus Lemma Candidates

Version: v2.2

This document introduces surplus lemma candidates.

Surplus measures positive margin after compensation.

## Lemma candidate 1: positive surplus

Native object:

surplus

Candidate statement:

For a compensated interval K, define:

S(K) = average discharge over K - log2(3)

If S(K) > 0, the interval has positive surplus.

Native meaning retained:

positive compensation margin after repair.

Status:

stable lemma candidate.

## Lemma candidate 2: zero or negative surplus

Native object:

insufficient compensation

Candidate statement:

If S(K) <= 0, the interval has not produced positive compensation surplus.

Native meaning retained:

repair did not exceed expansion pressure.

Status:

stable lemma candidate.

## Lemma candidate 3: positive surplus is not full closure

Native object:

closure

Candidate statement:

Positive surplus is necessary for local recovery, but it is not sufficient for full native closure unless shadow erasure and regeneration tests also pass.

Native meaning retained:

positive margin is not automatically obstruction erasure.

Status:

boundary lemma candidate.

## Lemma candidate 4: tight surplus identifies stress

Native object:

hardness

Candidate statement:

A very small positive surplus identifies structural stress, but not obstruction.

Native meaning retained:

tight recovery is hardness, not native failure.

Status:

boundary lemma candidate.

## Lemma candidate 5: surplus supports evidence mapping

Native object:

evidence layer

Candidate statement:

Positive surplus in finite scans supports local recovery evidence, but does not establish global closure.

Native meaning retained:

finite evidence remains evidence, not proof.

Status:

evidence boundary lemma candidate.

## Current status

Surplus is stable as a local measurement.

Its role in full closure remains dependent on shadow and regeneration.

tight positive surplus identifies structural stress, but not obstruction
