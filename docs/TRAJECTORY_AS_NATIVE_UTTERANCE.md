# Trajectory as Native Utterance

Version: v3.3

A trajectory is treated here as an utterance of the Collatz grammar.

This means the trajectory is not first interpreted as a route to 1.

It is interpreted as a generated sequence of native structural events.

## Classical reading

The classical reading asks:

Does this reach 1?

## Native reading

The native reading asks:

What does this trajectory produce, preserve, regenerate, lose, or close?

## Native utterance

A native utterance may contain:

- expansion
- discharge
- debt
- compensation
- shadow
- regeneration
- dangerous regeneration
- closure candidate
- obstruction candidate

## Important distinction

Reaching 1 is a terminal translation.

Native utterance is structural description before terminal translation.
